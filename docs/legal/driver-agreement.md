## 1. Relationship of the Parties

### 1.1 Independent contractor

Driver is engaged by DankDash as an **independent
contractor**, not as an employee, agent, partner, joint
venturer, or franchisee. Nothing in this Agreement creates
an employer-employee relationship.

### 1.2 Control over means and methods

Driver retains exclusive control over the means and methods
of performing deliveries, including:

- (a) when to log in to the DankDasher app and accept offers;
- (b) which delivery offers to accept or decline (Driver may
  decline any offer for any lawful reason);
- (c) what vehicle to use, subject to Section 4.1;
- (d) what route to drive, subject to lawful traffic rules
  and the geofence requirements of Minnesota cannabis law;
- (e) what hours and how many hours to work, subject to the
  legal hours-of-service limits of Minn. Stat. § 169.45 and
  related FMCSA-derived rules where applicable;
- (f) whether to work for competing delivery platforms
  (Driver is not subject to exclusivity).

DankDash provides operational parameters required by
Minnesota cannabis law (delivery time windows, geofence
boundaries, ID-scan requirements, Metrc reporting) but does
not direct the manner of Driver's work beyond what the law
itself requires.

### 1.3 No employee benefits

Driver is **not** entitled to any benefits that DankDash
provides to its employees, including health insurance, paid
time off, retirement plans, workers' compensation (except
where required by Minnesota law for delivery contractors —
see Section 8), or unemployment insurance.

### 1.4 Taxes and withholding

Driver is solely responsible for all federal, state, and
local income taxes, self-employment taxes, and any other
taxes arising from the compensation paid under this
Agreement. DankDash will issue an IRS Form 1099-NEC for each
calendar year in which Driver earns the reporting threshold
or more. DankDash does **not** withhold income tax,
FICA, or Medicare from Driver's payments.

## 3. Deliveries

### 3.1 Offer and acceptance

DankDash will surface delivery offers to active Drivers
through the DankDasher app. Each offer includes the pickup
dispensary, estimated route, estimated payout, and estimated
duration. You may accept or decline each offer at your sole
discretion.

Once you accept an offer, you become responsible for
completing the delivery in accordance with this Agreement
and Minnesota cannabis law unless and until the offer is
released back to the DankDash dispatch system through the
in-app **Release** function.

### 3.2 Pickup

At pickup, you must:

- (a) present your DankDash driver credentials and government
  ID to the Dispensary Partner staff;
- (b) confirm the Metrc package tag on each item matches the
  manifest;
- (c) confirm the order count;
- (d) sign the digital chain-of-custody record in the
  DankDasher app.

If the Metrc tag does not match the manifest, the order
**must not** be accepted. Report the discrepancy in-app and
release the offer.

### 3.3 Transport

While in transit:

- (a) cannabis product must remain in the locked compartment
  of the vehicle (trunk, locked cargo area, or
  manufacturer-installed locking glove box for vehicles
  without trunks `[FACT-CHECK — verify against current OCM
rule]`);
- (b) you must not consume cannabis or any impairing
  substance (zero tolerance — operating a vehicle while
  impaired is a per-se breach of this Agreement);
- (c) you must not allow any unauthorized person to access
  the cannabis product;
- (d) you must follow all traffic laws and lawful directions
  of law enforcement;
- (e) you must take only direct, lawful routes consistent
  with the delivery time window;
- (f) you may not cross state lines with cannabis product
  (federal prohibition — see also the dispatch system's
  geofence enforcement at the addressing layer).

### 3.4 Handoff

At the delivery address:

- (a) you must scan the customer's government-issued ID
  using the in-app Veriff integration;
- (b) you must visually confirm that the photo on the ID
  matches the recipient;
- (c) you must confirm the recipient is at least 21 years of
  age;
- (d) you must record the 6-digit handoff verification code
  exchanged with the customer;
- (e) you must confirm the order against the manifest before
  releasing it to the recipient;
- (f) if the recipient appears visibly intoxicated, you must
  refuse delivery and follow the refused-handoff procedure
  in the app.

The successful ID scan is **non-bypassable**. The order
state machine on the DankDash server will not permit a
`delivered` state without a Veriff session reference. Any
attempt to bypass this requirement is a material breach of
this Agreement and Minnesota cannabis law.

### 3.5 Refused or undeliverable orders

If the recipient is unavailable, refuses to verify ID, fails
ID verification, or refuses the delivery, you must:

- (a) record the refusal reason in-app;
- (b) return the product to the originating Dispensary
  Partner within the time window specified by the app;
- (c) re-confirm the Metrc tags at the return scan.

You will be compensated for refused or undeliverable orders
according to the payout structure in Section 5.

## 5. Compensation

### 5.1 Payout structure

For each delivery you complete, you will be paid the offered
payout shown at the time of offer acceptance. The payout
consists of:

- **Base fare** — a per-trip amount based on the dispatch
  zone;
- **Distance pay** — a per-mile amount for the route from
  pickup to drop-off;
- **Time pay** — a per-minute amount for the estimated
  trip duration;
- **Promotional adjustments** — surge, peak-hour, weather,
  or other dispatch incentives where applicable;
- **Tips** — 100% of any tip paid by the customer is yours;
  DankDash does not deduct service fees or transaction fees
  from tips.

For refused or undeliverable orders, you will be paid the
base fare plus distance pay for the route actually driven
(pickup-to-attempt + attempt-to-return).

### 5.2 Payment schedule

Payouts are settled weekly. The pay period closes at 11:59 PM
Central Time on Sunday. Payment is initiated by ACH transfer
to your linked Aeropay account within three (3) business days
following pay period close. You may also opt into instant
payout (subject to Aeropay's instant-payout fee — paid by
you, not DankDash) at any time during the pay period.

A pay-period statement is available in the DankDasher app
within 24 hours of pay period close. Statements show each
delivery, the components of each payout, any adjustments, and
the net amount paid. Disputes regarding pay must be raised
within 30 days of pay period close (Section 5.4).

### 5.3 Deductions

DankDash does **not** deduct from your payouts except for:

- (a) recoupment of provable overpayments (a clearly
  documented duplicate payment, a payout for a cancelled
  order, etc.);
- (b) settlement of customer refunds where you are at fault
  for the refund (delivered wrong order, damaged product,
  failed handoff verification — subject to the dispute
  process in Section 5.4);
- (c) recoupment of advances or other amounts you have
  expressly authorized in writing.

All other operating costs — gas, vehicle maintenance,
insurance, mobile phone, mobile data — are yours.

### 5.4 Pay disputes

If you believe a payout is incorrect, file a dispute in-app
or by emailing drivers@dankdash.com within thirty (30) days
of pay period close. DankDash will review and respond within
ten (10) business days. The dispute resolution process for
unresolved pay disputes follows Section 12.

### 5.5 No guaranteed earnings

DankDash does **not** guarantee any minimum number of
offers, any minimum number of deliveries, or any minimum
weekly or hourly earnings. Earnings vary by demand, time of
day, location, your acceptance rate, and other factors. Any
"earnings estimate" surfaced in the app or in marketing
material is an estimate, not a guarantee.

## 6. Compliance Obligations

### 6.1 Minnesota cannabis law

You acknowledge that you are performing deliveries of
adult-use cannabis under Minn. Stat. § 342 and that you
have read, understood, and agree to follow:

- Minn. Stat. § 342.27 (sale limits);
- Minn. Rule 21-23 (delivery and recordkeeping);
- OCM bulletins and notices applicable to delivery personnel
  (subscribe at `ocm.mn.gov/bulletins`);
- The Driver Compliance Handbook, available in the DankDasher
  app under Settings → Compliance Handbook (the
  **"Handbook"**). The Handbook may be updated from time to
  time; you agree to read updates within seven (7) days of
  release.

### 6.2 Federal law

You acknowledge that cannabis remains a Schedule I controlled
substance under federal law, that this creates legal risks
that DankDash cannot eliminate, and that crossing any state
border with cannabis product is a federal offense. You agree
not to do so.

### 6.3 Recordkeeping and audits

You agree to cooperate with internal DankDash compliance
reviews and with external OCM audits. Cooperation includes:

- (a) producing your delivery log, vehicle records, and
  insurance certificate on request;
- (b) participating in interviews;
- (c) preserving records for the audit period specified by
  Minn. Rule 21-23 (currently seven years).

Your delivery records and chain-of-custody signatures are
maintained by DankDash and are available to OCM under our
license-compliance-audit runbook.

### 6.4 Substance use

You agree:

- (a) **not to consume cannabis or any other impairing
  substance** during a shift (from the time you log in
  through the time you log out);
- (b) not to operate a vehicle within four (4) hours of
  consuming cannabis or any impairing substance;
- (c) not to deliver to a recipient who appears visibly
  impaired by alcohol or other substances;
- (d) to submit to a reasonable-suspicion impairment
  assessment if a DankDash compliance officer requests one
  following an incident.

`[REVIEW WITH COUNSEL]` — Minnesota's adult-use cannabis law
treats employee/contractor drug testing differently than
many states, and the substance-use clause must align with
both that statute and the recently amended Minn. Stat. §
181.951 lawful-consumable-products protections. The "no
impairing substance during shift" language should be
operationally defensible without crossing into
unenforceable territory.

### 6.5 Incidents and reportable events

You must report the following to DankDash within one (1)
hour of occurrence, via the in-app Incident Report screen:

- (a) any vehicle accident involving cannabis product on
  board, regardless of fault;
- (b) any theft, loss, or suspected theft of cannabis
  product;
- (c) any law-enforcement stop or inspection during a
  delivery;
- (d) any customer complaint regarding handoff (refused ID,
  perceived impairment, aggression, etc.);
- (e) any injury sustained during a delivery;
- (f) any incident triggering the
  `customer-complaint-escalation` runbook on the DankDash
  side (you may not know which, but report and let the
  compliance team triage).

Failure to report a reportable event is a material breach.

### 9.1 Term

You may terminate this Agreement at any time, for any

## 11. Limitation of Liability

aggregate liability to Driver under or in connection with
this Agreement is limited to the greater of (a) the total
compensation paid to Driver in the twelve (12) months

(USD $1,000.00).

### 11.2 Excluded damages

Neither Party is liable for indirect, incidental, special,
consequential, exemplary, or punitive damages arising out
of or in connection with this Agreement, including lost
profits or lost earnings.

### 11.3 Carve-outs

The cap and the excluded-damages limitation in Sections
11.1 and 11.2 do **not** apply to:

- (a) a Party's indemnification obligations under Section 10;
- (b) Driver's breach of Section 3.3(b) (impairment), Section
  3.4 (handoff), Section 6 (compliance), or Section 7.1

## 12. Dispute Resolution

### 12.1 Internal escalation

Before initiating any formal proceeding, the Parties agree
to attempt good-faith resolution by:

- (a) the initiating Party providing written notice of the
  dispute and proposed resolution to the other Party;
- (b) a thirty (30) day period during which DankDash's
  driver-relations team and Driver attempt to resolve the
  dispute by direct communication.

This internal step does not apply where injunctive relief
is sought to prevent imminent harm.

### 12.2 Binding arbitration

If internal escalation does not resolve the dispute, the
dispute will be resolved by binding individual arbitration
administered by JAMS under its Streamlined Arbitration
Rules and Procedures, applying Minnesota substantive law.
The arbitration will be conducted in Hennepin County,
Minnesota, or remotely at the parties' election. Each
Party bears its own attorneys' fees except as the
arbitrator may award under applicable law.

### 12.3 Class action waiver

You and DankDash each agree that disputes will be resolved
only on an individual basis and not as part of a class,
collective, or representative action. The arbitrator may
not consolidate claims of more than one individual or
preside over any form of representative or class
proceeding.

`[REVIEW WITH COUNSEL]` — the enforceability of class
action waivers in independent-contractor agreements has
been litigated heavily since _Epic Systems v. Lewis_ (2018)
and remains contested for PAGA-style representative claims
under state law. Minnesota analogue (Minn. Stat. § 177.27)
should be analyzed.

### 12.4 Carve-out for small-claims

Notwithstanding Section 12.2, either Party may bring a
claim qualifying for small-claims-court jurisdiction in
the small-claims court of Hennepin County, Minnesota, on
an individual basis.

### 12.5 Right to opt out of arbitration

You may opt out of the arbitration provisions of Sections
12.2 and 12.3 by notifying DankDash in writing within
thirty (30) days of your acceptance of this Agreement.
The opt-out notice must be sent to legal@dankdash.com
with the subject line "Driver Arbitration Opt-Out" and
must include your full legal name, DankDasher email, and
the statement "I opt out of the arbitration provisions of
the DankDasher Independent Contractor Agreement." Opting
out does not affect any other provision of this Agreement.

`[REVIEW WITH COUNSEL]` — the opt-out window is included
to reduce enforceability risk; thirty (30) days is the
industry-standard window.
